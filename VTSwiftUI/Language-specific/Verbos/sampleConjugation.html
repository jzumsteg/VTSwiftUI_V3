This sample conjugation, based on the word "comer -- to eat" gives you an idea of the verb conjugations. Spanish verb conjugations and their equivalents in English are much more complicated than we can show here. I refer you to any good Spanish verb book for more complete explanations.
presente
yo como  I eat/am eating.
tú comes  You (inf.) eat/are eating.
el; ella; Ud. come  You (formal)/he/she eats; is eating.
nosotros comemos  We eat/are eating.
vosotros comeis  You (inf.) eat/are eating.
ellos;ellas;Uds. comen  They eat/are eating.
vos comés  You eat/are eating.
imperfecto
yo comía  I was eating.
tú comías  You (inf.) were eating.
el; ella; Ud. comía  You (formal)/he/she was eating.
nosotros comíamos  We were eating.
vosotros comíais  You (pl. inf.) were eating.
ellos;ellas;Uds. comían  They were eating.
vos comías  You (sing. inf.) were eating.

tú comiste  You (inf.) ate

nosotros comimos  We ate.
vosotros comisteis  You (pl. Inf.) ate.
ellos;ellas;Uds. comieron  They ate.
vos comiste  You (sing. inf.) ate.
futuro

vos comerás  They will eat.

vos comerías  You (inf.) would eat..

perfecto de indicativo

pluscuamperfecto de indic.
Used to indicate an action in the past that occurred before another action.
yo había  ...before, I had eaten.
tú habías  ...before, you (inf.) had eaten.
el; ella; Ud.habíia  ...before, you (formal)/he/she had eaten.
nosotros habíamos  ...before, we had eaten.
vosotros habíais  ...before, you (pl. Inf.) had eaten.
ellos;ellas;Uds. habíian  ...before, they had eaten.
"...before" indicates a phrase of something that happened before: "Before my brother arrived, I had eaten."
pretérito anterior
Seldom used in spoken Spanish. Used after a conjunction of time: después de, cuando, etc.
yo hube comido  ...after I had eaten.
tú hubiste comido  ...after you (inf.) had eaten.
el; ella; Ud. hubo comido  ...after you (formal)/he/she had eaten.
nosotros hubimos comido  ...after we had eaten.
vosotros hubisteis comido  ...after you (pl. Inf.) had eaten.
ellos;ellas;Uds. hubieron comido  ...after they had eaten.
"...after" indicates a conjunction of time.
vos hubiste...after you (inf.) had eaten.
futuro perfecto
Used to indicate an action in the future that will occur before another action in the future.
yo habré  I will have eaten before....
tú habrás  You (inf.) will have eaten before...
el; ella; Ud. habrá  You (formal)/he/she will have eaten before...
nosotros habremos  We will have eaten before...
vosotros habéis  You (pl. Inf.) will have eaten before...
ellos;ellas;Uds. habrán  They will have eaten before...
vos habrás  You (inf.) will have eaten before...
before... indicates an action that will occur in the future after the action described by the futuro perfecto verb phrase: "I will have eaten before my brother arrives.
condicional compuesto
Used to indicate an action in the past that would have occurred if another action or condition had occurred.
yo habré comido  I would have eaten if...
tú habrás comido  You (inf.) I would have eaten if...
el; ella; Ud. habrá comido  You (formal)/he/she I would have eaten if...
nosotros habremos comido  We I would have eaten if...
vosotros habréis comido  You (pl. Inf.) I would have eaten if...
ellos;ellas;Uds. habrán comido  They I would have eaten if...
vos habrás comido  You (inf.) I would have eaten if...
if... indicates the action or condition: "I would have eaten if I had had enough money.
